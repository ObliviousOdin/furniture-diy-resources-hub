# Furniture DIY Resources Hub

**Curated, free, and open resources for furniture assembly, packing (moving/shipping), and repair/refinishing.**

A living, self-evolving knowledge base. Started with comprehensive seed content and designed for continuous improvement by AI agents like Hermes.

## Overview

This repository compiles the best freely available manuals, step-by-step guides, search strategies, and community resources for:

- **Assembly**: Manufacturer instructions, general guides, SOPs.
- **Packing & Moving**: Disassembly, protection, and transport best practices.
- **Repair & Restoration**: Fixing, refinishing, reupholstering wood and other furniture.

**Why this repo?** Scattered resources across the web make it hard to find reliable info. Here, everything is organized, searchable, and actively maintained/evolved.

## Core Resources (Seed Content)

### 1. Internet Archive (Strongest Broad Collection)

The Internet Archive remains one of the best places for furniture-related manuals and repair books. It includes:

- Specific brand assembly instructions (e.g., South Shore Furniture PDFs).
- Classic repair books like Reader’s Digest Fix-It-Yourself Manual (covers furniture repair, refinishing, and reupholstering).
- Military/technical manuals such as TM 5-613 Woodworking and Furniture Repair.
- General furniture repair & restoration guides with projects and methods.

**Search tips:** Go to archive.org and search terms like:
- “furniture assembly instructions”
- “furniture repair”
- “woodworking furniture repair”
- Specific brands (e.g., “South Shore Furniture assembly”)
- “furniture refinishing” or “reupholstering”

Many items are downloadable as PDFs and in the public domain or freely contributed.

### 2. iFixit – Furniture and Décor Repair Guides (Best for Repair)

iFixit hosts a dedicated, community-driven section for Furniture and Décor Repair with free step-by-step guides. Examples include:

- Reattaching broken wood pieces.
- Fixing chipped wood furniture.
- Drawer track repairs.
- General techniques for wood furniture.

**Link:** [ifixit.com/Device/Furniture](https://ifixit.com/Device/Furniture)

They also cover broader household/DIY repairs. This is one of the most practical “open repair” resources available—similar to open source in spirit, with clear instructions and photos.

### 3. Manufacturer & Brand-Specific Assembly Manuals

Many furniture companies provide free official PDFs:

- **IKEA**: Searchable database of assembly instructions for nearly all products. → [ikea.com](https://www.ikea.com) (product pages or dedicated assembly guide section).
- Other brands: KI Furniture, HPFi, Silva Furniture, South Shore, etc., often have assembly/maintenance PDFs on their sites (search “[brand] assembly instructions”).

These are excellent for precise, model-specific assembly.

### 4. Aggregator Sites for Manuals

- **ManualsLib.com**: Massive library (millions of manuals) — search for furniture brands/models.
- **ManyManuals.com**: Another solid user manual repository.

### 5. GitHub & Open/DIY Projects

Fewer centralized “open source SOPs” for furniture work compared to software, but some useful resources exist:

- Open source furniture projects (e.g., parametric/CNC designs that include assembly instructions).
- Research-oriented repos like IKEA manual alignments with videos (more technical but interesting for understanding assembly processes).
- DIY woodworking plans and custom furniture build guides (search GitHub for “open source furniture” or “DIY furniture plans”).

For packing: Look for general “furniture moving/packing guides” or disassembly instructions (often paired with assembly manuals from manufacturers). Internet Archive and moving/DIY sites have practical tips on protecting and packing furniture for transport.

## Quick Notes on SOPs vs. Manuals

- Manuals & step-by-step guides are abundant (especially via Archive + iFixit + brands).
- Dedicated open source SOP collections specifically for furniture assembly/packing/repair are limited—most physical work processes live in individual guides, manufacturer docs, or DIY books rather than large aggregated repos.

## Recommended Starting Points

1. Search Internet Archive for broad coverage.
2. Check iFixit Furniture section for repair fixes.
3. Use IKEA (or brand sites) for assembly PDFs.
4. Cross-reference with ManualsLib for hard-to-find models.
